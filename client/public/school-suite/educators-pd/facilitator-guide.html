Teaching for the Discernment Age

Everything needed to run the day: the run-sheet, learning outcomes, NZ alignment, logistics, pricing, and the pre-delivery currency check. Written to be handed to a school and delivered tomorrow.

1 · The one-sentence pitch

Every AI course for schools teaches tools and policies that date within a term. This one builds the thing that doesn't — teacher discernment — and turns it into a discernment practice students carry into any subject, tool, or year. Formation-first, with a real tools layer on top.

The hidden spine (faith layer)The whole day rests on one move no other school AI-PD makes: worship-shaped discernment — you discern best what you have learned to love rightly (EDU-35, Prov 9:10) — and a teacher can only pass on the discernment they have first practised (EDU-14, Study–Obey–Teach). On the state-school toggle, this reads simply as "discernment is trained at the level of values, and modelled before it's taught."

2 · Learning outcomes

Each teacher will be able to…

  • Run and teach the six-step VERIFY drill on any AI output
  • Apply the green/amber/red model to their own assessments
  • Name which cognitive stages AI skips in their subject
  • State a defensible position on assessment integrity
  • Model ordered AI use before prescribing students'

The school will leave with…

  • A staff-authored, signed AI covenant on the wall
  • A shared cross-department language for AI use
  • A "for parents" one-pager to send home that week
  • A community-set line on academic integrity
  • Each teacher's "protected struggle" list per subject

4 · The four frameworks (what's yours, cited faithfully)

  • Study → Obey → Teach — the teacher-formation loop as an anti-outsourcing method. EDU-14 · Ezra 7:10-11
  • VERIFY — Verify the claim · Examine the source · Research independently · Identify bias · Form a confidence rating · Your verdict. Full pupil worksheet exists at ../worksheets/Edu_Worksheet_1_VERIFY_Method.html
  • The Traffic-Light model — green / amber / red as a shared AI-use language, applied to real assignments.
  • Worship-shaped discernment — critical thinking trained at the level of what a student loves, not just what they can fact-check. EDU-35 · Prov 9:10

6 · NZ curriculum & professional alignment

  • Teaching Council — Standards for the Profession: professional learning; fostering a learning-focused culture; design for learning.
  • The NZ Curriculum key competencies: Thinking; Managing self; Relating to others — the discernment practice targets these directly.
  • Digital Technologies / digital fluency: critical, ethical use of digital tools.
  • NCEA authenticity & assessment integrity: Session 3 gives staff a community-owned position, not a detection dependency.
  • Netsafe / MoE digital citizenship: complements existing guidance with a formation frame.

7 · Pre-delivery currency checklist

NCEA, Ministry and Netsafe positions shift quickly. Run this in the week before every delivery.

  • Confirm current NZQA/NCEA position on AI use & authenticity — update any cited wording.
  • Check the latest Ministry of Education AI-in-schools guidance for changes.
  • Check the current Netsafe schools position and any new resources to signpost.
  • Confirm the school's own existing AI/device policy — the covenant sits alongside it, not against it.
  • Confirm register with the principal: state (faith layer off) or faith-integrated (faith layer on).
  • Re-verify every devotion anchor against devotions/education.json (§5).
  • Refresh any statistic against presentations/stats-dashboard-updated.html.
