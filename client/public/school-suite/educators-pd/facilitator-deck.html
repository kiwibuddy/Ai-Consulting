Teaching for the
Discernment Age

Information became free. Discernment became scarce. What is our teaching actually for now?

The day at a glance

Four sessions, one spine

  • Session 1 — The telos question. What is school for? And the teacher's own formation.
  • Session 2 — Discernment as practice. VERIFY & the traffic light, hands-on.
  • Session 3 — The classroom reality. Assessment integrity & protecting struggle.
  • Session 4 — The staffroom covenant. Three norms we'll actually keep.

Full day = 6 hours with breaks · or two 90-minute twilight sessions (1–2, then 3–4).

Session 1 · 75 min

The telos question

What is school for when information is free?

The shift

The task changed underneath us

The old task

Get scarce information into students' heads. Transmit, test, repeat.

The new task

Form the discernment that decides what to trust and what to do with information that is now everywhere and free.

"The risk isn't AI adoption. The risk is accidental adoption — without discernment."

Foundations · Education sphere · EDU-08, EDU-10, EDU-12Scripture never made information the goal. The purpose of instruction is transformed lives and a transformed society (1 Tim 1:5; Deut 5:29-31; Lev 18:1-5). When information is free, that older telos is the only one worth building a school on.
Turn it on ourselves first

Study → Obey → Teach

You cannot teach what you have not practised. You cannot practise by proxy.

  • Study — understand the tool honestly, not fearfully.
  • Obey — order your own AI use before you prescribe your students'.
  • Teach — now you have something real to pass on.
Anchor · EDU-14 · Ezra 7:10-11Ezra "set his heart to study… to do… and to teach." The order is the method — and it is the opposite of outsourcing.
Exercise · 15 min

Your own AI-use audit

Workbook, page 2. Privately and honestly: where do you already reach for the machine first? Where has it quietly changed how you prepare, mark, or think? No sharing required — this is between you and your own practice.

  • What do I use AI for in my teaching right now?
  • What am I modelling to students when I do?
  • What one habit would I want a student to copy — or never copy?
Session 2 · 90 min

Discernment as a teachable practice

From "be careful online" to a drill students can actually run.

The core tool · hands-on

The VERIFY method

Six steps students run on any AI output — a repeatable drill, not a vibe. We'll do it live on a real example.

V
Verify the claim
E
Examine the source
R
Research independently
I
Identify bias / motive
F
Form a confidence rating
Y
Your verdict
Anchor · EDU-33, EDU-35"The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom" (Prov 9:10). Worship-shaped discernment — you discern best what you have learned to love rightly. This is more than fact-checking.
Live example · NZ

Run VERIFY on this

Ask an AI: "Did the English and Māori versions of the Treaty of Waitangi say the same thing?" Then run all six steps.

  • How many separate claims are hidden in its answer?
  • What authoritative NZ source could actually settle it? (Te Ara, Waitangi Tribunal, Te Papa)
  • Is the error a factual mistake — or a bias problem?

This is the difference between "AI is sometimes wrong" and a student who can show exactly where and why.

The shared language

The Traffic-Light model

One school-wide vocabulary for AI use — applied to your assignments, not a generic policy.

🟢 GreenUse freely. AI genuinely helps and the learning is elsewhere. (e.g. brainstorming topics, checking spelling.)
🟠 AmberUse with permission & disclosure. The tool touches the learning — declare how you used it. (e.g. structuring an argument.)
🔴 RedOff-limits. Using AI here skips the exact thing being formed. (e.g. the first draft you're meant to think through.)

Exercise: take one real assignment. Colour each part. Where's the red — and why?

Session 3 · 90 min

The classroom reality

Assessment integrity, and protecting struggle on purpose.

The honest position

Detection loses the arms race

Watermark-defeat is real. AI-detectors produce false positives. You cannot software your way to integrity.

  • The community, not the tool, sets the non-negotiable standard.
  • Integrity is a shared culture staff hold — not a scanner they run.
  • Design assessment so the learning happens where AI can't reach.
Anchor · EDU-21 · James 3:1Teachers are "judged by a higher standard." The community's held standard, not a detection score, is what forms honest students.
Which stages does AI skip?

Protecting the struggle that forms

Map your subject up the ladder. AI most wants to skip the middle — where thinking is actually built. Mark the rungs worth protecting.

L1 Remembering — recall facts
L2 Understanding — explain in own words  ⚠️
L3 Applying — use it in a new case  ⚠️
L4 Analysing — break down, compare  ⚠️
L5 Evaluating — judge, defend a view
L6 Creating — make something new
Anchor · EDU-41–46, EDU-29Scripture treats remembering, understanding, applying, analysing, evaluating and creating as distinct works of a formed mind — the very stages a shortcut deletes. "Press on… press on… press on" (Hosea 6:3).
Session 4 · 45 min

The staffroom covenant

Not a policy handed down. Three norms this staff writes — and keeps.

The closing artifact

Three shared AI norms

In table groups, then together: draft three plain commitments — in your own words — that this staff will hold each other to. Then sign the poster.

  • What will we always do? (e.g. "We disclose our own AI use to students.")
  • What will we never do? (e.g. "We never mark work we didn't design for AI use.")
  • What will we decide together, not alone?
Anchor · EDU-19, EDU-40Formation is communal. A relational standard the whole staff owns outlasts any rule from above — and honours the teachers who keep it.
The charge

Build people, not just filters.

The age doesn't need better detectors first. It needs formed people — students who can tell the true from the plausible, and act well on the difference. That has always been our job. Now it's the only job.

The whole day in one lineWorship-shaped discernment — and a school that forms it is doing the oldest, deepest work there is.
