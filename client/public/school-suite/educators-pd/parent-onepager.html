AI, wisdom & your child

Our staff spent a professional-development day on this. Here's what we're teaching — and three things you can do at home.

Your child is growing up in a world where information is free and instant — and where a confident wrong answer, a fake photo, or an essay they didn't write is only a tap away. The skill that matters most now isn't finding information. It's wisdom: knowing what to trust, and what to do with it. That's a skill you help build at home more than anywhere else.

What the school is teaching

We're helping students build discernment as a real, practised skill — a simple method for checking any AI answer (we call it VERIFY), a shared "traffic-light" language for when AI is fine, when it needs permission, and when it's off-limits, and a commitment to protect the honest effort that actually builds a mind. We're not banning AI or fearing it. We're teaching students to be the kind of person who uses it well.

Three things that help at home

🗣️

Ask before you outsource

When your child reaches for an app, ask what they think first. The pause is where wisdom grows.

Try: "What's your best guess, before we check?"
🔎

Check one thing together

Next time AI gives an answer, verify one claim together. Model that confident ≠ correct.

Try: "Where would we find out if that's true?"
🛑

Protect the struggle

Some effort is the point — the reading, the first draft, the hard maths. Let them wrestle before the tool helps.

Try: "Do this bit yourself first — that's the learning."

Good questions to ask any app — or your child about it

  • What is this teaching me to want or reach for first?
  • Who am I becoming when I use it a lot?
  • What can I do that it can't — and am I still practising that?
