Two delivery tracks

Three footprints: full year, semester, or a 10-week pilot

Same spine, same outcomes framework — two footprints. The full year runs 2–3 timetabled hours per week across four terms. The semester intensive runs 4–5 hours per week across two terms. The Pilot Term compresses the spine into one 10-week term — the lowest-risk way for a school to trial the programme with a single class before scaling up.

Every track ends in a public Showcase — a full evening for the year and semester tracks, a class-scale Mini-Showcase for the Pilot Term — students present their portfolio to whānau, staff and local employers.

Built for the NZ system — through the reform and beyond

📐 Curriculum & qualifications

  • Designed to sit alongside NCEA Levels 2–3 study — a timetabled elective, mentoring line, or enrichment block
  • Portfolio artefacts are structured so many can double as evidence toward relevant internal standards — confirmed with your HoDs and principal's nominee
  • Explicitly maps to the NZC key competencies: thinking; relating to others; using language, symbols and texts; managing self; participating and contributing
  • Future-proof: as NZCE and NZACE arrive from 2029–2030, evidenced capability is the constant that survives the change

🧭 Pathways & careers

  • Compatible with Vocational Pathways, and pairs naturally with Gateway and STAR placements for the embodied-skill tier
  • Integrates with students' Tahatū career planning — the portfolio gives their profile something real to show
  • Local employer partners invited to Showcase Night — the warm-introduction channel that beats the CV black hole
  • Whānau engagement built in: two family touchpoints per track, because pathway decisions happen at kitchen tables

🤝 Values & special character

  • The Values Spine uses universal principle language — integrity, service, stewardship, courage, restraint, gratitude — accessible to every student
  • Fully adaptable: state schools run it as-is against their school values; special-character schools can map each weekly theme to their own tradition and texts
  • Nothing in the student experience assumes or requires any faith position — and nothing in it prevents a school from adding one
  • Aligned with wellbeing priorities: identity is anchored in who students are becoming, not in a volatile job market

🧑‍🏫 Staffing & workload

  • Any capable teacher, dean or careers lead can facilitate — no specialist AI or business background needed
  • Every session arrives planned: facilitator guide, slides, timing, printables and assessment rubrics included
  • One 3-hour staff PLD workshop before launch; optional termly check-ins
  • Designed to reduce marking load, not add to it: portfolio evidence is authenticated, not re-marked
