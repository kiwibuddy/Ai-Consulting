Section 1 · Context
What's actually happening in our region
Before you assess yourself, it's worth knowing what's happening around you. The Bay of Plenty is not behind.

The Port of Tauranga is already using AI for health and safety monitoring. Zespri and PlantTech are running AI-driven yield prediction trials. The Tauranga Business Chamber ran its first dedicated AI for Business session in 2025. Priority One has flagged AI adoption as central to the region's shift toward a higher-wage, knowledge-intensive economy.

None of that means you're behind. It means the conversation is real and local. What is true is that the gap between large businesses and small ones is wide, and the government has noticed.

67%
large biz
Large business AI usage jumped from 48% to 67% in 2024. SME adoption is still in single digits in many sectors. That gap is exactly why the government launched a co-funded AI advisory programme this year.
Source: MBIE / AI New Zealand, 2024–2025
Worth knowing
The AI Advisory Pilot — is your business eligible?
In January 2026, the Government launched a $765,000 co-funded AI advisory pilot through the Regional Business Partner Network. At least 51 SMEs will receive funded support to develop an AI adoption plan. The entry point in our region is Priority One. This worksheet will help you get clear on where you stand before you have that conversation.
Step 1.1

Before moving into the self-assessment, spend two minutes thinking honestly about this question:

When you hear "AI for small business," what's your gut reaction?

Your answer here doesn't change what you'll find in the worksheet. It just helps calibrate how you read the results.

Why this question first
The biggest barrier to AI adoption in NZ isn't technology. MBIE's own research identifies lack of expertise and awareness as the primary blockers for small businesses. Your starting mindset matters more than your starting knowledge.

Section 2 · Tools audit
What are you already using?
Many business owners are already using AI without labelling it that way. This section makes it visible.

If you've used a chatbot on a website, asked Siri a question, had Facebook suggest who to tag in a photo, or used Google Translate, you've used AI. The question is whether you're using it intentionally for your business — and whether those tools are doing real work for you.

Step 2.1
Check what's already in your toolkit

Go through this list. Mark the ones that are actually part of how you work. Be honest — "I signed up once" doesn't count.

ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini
General AI assistants. Used for writing, research, summarising, answering questions.
Canva AI / Adobe Firefly
AI-assisted design tools. Useful for marketing content and social posts.
Xero / MYOB smart tools
Both platforms now include AI-assisted expense categorisation and forecasting.
Google Analytics / Meta Ads
AI is baked into how both platforms optimise and report. You're already using it.
Shopify / WooCommerce AI
Product descriptions, pricing suggestions, inventory alerts — all AI-assisted.
Calendly / scheduling tools
Smart scheduling and automated follow-up often includes AI components.
Zoom / Teams AI notes
Meeting summaries and action item extraction. Increasingly the default.
What this tells you
If you marked more than two of these, you're already an AI user. The question isn't whether to adopt AI — it's whether you're getting intentional value from what you've already got.

Most guides ask "what could AI help you with?" The better question is: what tasks do you do every week that you'd rather hand off? What takes you an hour that probably shouldn't? What do you dread because it's tedious, not because it's hard?

Section 4 · Barriers
What's actually stopping you?
The barriers to AI adoption for small businesses are well-documented. Knowing yours helps you find the right support.

MBIE surveyed NZ small businesses about why they haven't adopted AI. The top answer — cited by 43% of non-users — was lack of expertise and awareness. Not cost. Not privacy. Not technology access. Just not knowing where to start.

Step 4.1
Name your actual barrier
What is genuinely holding you back from using AI more deliberately in your business?
On the "it doesn't apply to me" response
This is worth taking seriously rather than dismissing. The honest answer is: AI applies differently to different businesses. A Tauranga kiwifruit exporter, a Mount Maunganui hospitality operator, and a tradesperson working across the Western Bay have genuinely different use cases. The question is always which specific tasks, not whether AI "applies" in general.
Step 4.2
Check your data and privacy awareness

NZ's Privacy Commissioner released updated AI guidance in 2025. Most small businesses have no idea it exists. Two minutes here could save a headache later.

Are you aware of any privacy obligations that come with using AI tools in your business?
Key thing to know
NZ's 13 Privacy Principles apply to any AI tool that collects or processes customer data. The Office of the Privacy Commissioner recommends conducting a Privacy Impact Assessment before deploying AI in customer-facing contexts. Worksheet 2 in this series covers this in plain language.
