Chapter 1
Who this playbook is for (and why it’s so long)
You’re not selling a SaaS dashboard. You’re selling Pacific-time expertise, a locally salted curriculum, and tiered reassurance—with software doing only what’s worth automating: payment and emailed links.

You are the protagonist. Most “operator manuals” collapse because they assume you remember how Stripe metadata works three months later. This one doesn’t—it explains the causal chain slowly: buyer intent → Stripe Checkout → webhook → Resend → your calendar and your conscience.

Read one chapter before bed, skim another before a stressful client call. The goal is durable mental models, not brute memorisation. When you genuinely understand why each tier exists, you’ll stop improvising dangerously about the Government Pilot—or selling yourself short on Silver upsides.

How to squeeze value from this workbook
Print to PDF (“Save as PDF” from browser) once a quarter after you annotate the reflection boxes. Keep highlights where you stumble—that’s where your standard operating procedure is weak.

Chapter 2
What you sell—in one coherent story
Every artefact hangs off a narrative: SMEs need confidence before they adopt AI; confidence comes from truth about self (readiness, time map, culture, consent & law); delivered through guided tools + optional human translation.

Picture a worried owner staring at headlines about robots taking jobs. Their board member forwards an MBIE link. Someone mentions “Priority One” at Chamber breakfast. They feel late but don’t trust vendor demos. Your product intervenes exactly there: first with homework-like worksheets (emotionally manageable), then optional you who turns homework into commitments and tool choices—and at Gold tier, into a polished AI Adoption Plan that matches what funded advisory pathways expect as a tangible output.

You are positioning three different degrees of accompaniment:

  • Bronze (Self-Pack): “I'll do the introspection myself; mail me structured tools + Session 1’s evidence-rich deck.”
  • Silver (Implementation Pack): “I'll pay for someone bilingual in tools and NZ privacy risk to shorten my experimentation cycle.”
  • Gold (Adoption Plan): “I need institutional-grade documentation—and I likely want to finance part of your fee through Pilot alignment.”
Remember
The slides and worksheets are teaching devices; Stripe charges for assembled access + facilitation rights (recording, templates, drafting time)—not merely files they could pirate if they hunted URLs.

Chapter 3
Market · Bay of Plenty & New Zealand SMEs
You’ve anchored artefacts in credible local colour (horticulture, logistics, regional workforce stats). Buyers outside BoP still get legal truth on Privacy/Copyright—they’re buying rigorous NZ scaffolding with familiar regional anecdotes.

NZ SME reality (qualitative truths you preach with): scarce management bandwidth, scepticism about hype-cycle AI, fragmented tool adoption (“someone bought ChatGPT Business on a whim”), fear of messing up Māori workforce conversations, latent awareness that Pilot-style supports exist—but confusion about pathways.

Who fits Bronze vs Silver?

  • Solo ops & 1–10 staff: time-poor, needs speed and safety without meetings—Bronze shines if they honour self-guided follow-through.
  • 5–25 needing execution confidence: Silver’s 60 minutes compresses trial-and-error; email support turns edge-case fear into clarified next steps.
  • 10–50 with governance itch: board minutes will reference “risk register” someday—Gold legitimises experimentation with artefacts they can circulate internally.

Craft & construction micro-verticals. Trades often buy Bronze quietly (pride / “I'll figure tools myself”) unless a bookkeeper nags them—sell time saved in dollars, not jargon. Hospitality groups fear staff churn rumours if AI appears “replacement.” Professional services fret client confidentiality—that’s Legal worksheet territory before any Copilot rollout.

Signal: price sensitivity
They negotiate on Bronze only—offer clarity on Pilot upside without promising dollars; anchor value on advisory hours they'd otherwise buy blind.
Signal: compliance voice in room
Invite Silver early; bring PIA/policy language so GC or external accountant sleeps better.
Signal: Māori-majority workforce
Slow down; cite Team worksheet + eventual Te Tiriti checklist (Gold)—never tokenise urgency.

Chapter 5
The four curriculum lenses (worksheets × decks)
Each pillar answers a dominant anxiety; Session 1 is fully cited—later sessions aspire to parity.
  • Readiness lens: “Where genuinely are we emotionally + operationally?” (score, barriers, aspiration vs fear).
  • Time lens: “Which minutes leave the building weekly?” Turns AI into operational economics—not magic.
  • Culture lens: “Who feels exposed & honoured?” Gives managers guardrails vs performative inclusivity.
  • Risk lens (Legal): “Which inputs violate privacy or IP assumptions?” Gives compliance-oriented owners permission to scrutinise SaaS—not block innovation.

Worksheets ↔ decks ↔ paid tiers · how learning stacks

The worksheet is slow, handwritten thinking space; the slide deck lectures with social proof via citations (Session 1 is the gold standard today). Buyers on Bronze must feel Session 1 completes—not duplicates—the Readiness worksheet. Silver calls should reference specific slide numbers/modals where clients got stuck—that bonds them to evidence. Gold workshops can literally project Session 4 legal frames when aligning leadership.

When Session 2–4 decks grow to full bibliography parity, revise this playbook paragraph: parity means every bold stat survivors challenge you on has a clickable trail—your authority compounds.

Chapter 7
Government Pilot / Priority One framing
In code: marketing copy only. No MBIE handshake. Understand that boundary so clients don’t think “purchase = grant.”

Regional Business Partner Network + AI Advisory Pilot narrative is contextual oxygen: it motivates urgency—“someone subsidised diligence.” But disbursement pipelines have rules you don’t authenticate here. Speak in conditional language externally: eligibility is determined by advisers (e.g. Priority One)—your Gold tier aligns intellectually with Adoption Plan artefacts they may reimburse.

Safe external phrasing cheat line
“Paid materials prepare you with evidence-backed orientation; subsidy eligibility sits with authorised regional partners—we don’t adjudicate eligibility in checkout.” Recite calmly when challenged.

Money trail · Stripe vs Pilot rebate
Two parallel ledgers mentally: Stripe revenue certainty vs uncertain external reimbursement timelines.

Stripe deposits land fast; rebates or co-funds (if realised) reconcile later through your client’s application efforts. Pricing should already assume Pilot is not guaranteed—marketing may highlight upside, CFO brain must recognise cash still starts with your invoice.

Technical spine from click to inbox
High-level map so debugging doesn’t feel mystical.
  1. React sales page posts POST /api/public/products/checkout with JSON body e.g. {"tier":"silver"} (tier is bronze, silver, or gold).
  2. Server builds Checkout Session with metadata productSlug=tauranga-sme + tier; success URL hits /tauranga-sme/welcome.
  3. Stripe webhook checkout.session.completed → processProductCheckoutCompleted reads email + builds access links.
  4. taurangaAccessEmail goes to buyer; BCC to SITE_CONTACT_EMAIL.
  5. You react as human for higher tiers beyond template links.

Chapter 18
Silver · sixty-minute strategy call blueprint
Protect the transcript promise—structure beats rambling empathy.
  • Minutes 0–8 · Frame: confirm purchase expectations, confidentiality, consent to record.
  • Minutes 9–22 · Evidence tour: have them screenshare quickest wins from Readiness + Time Audit (even rough notes)—you annotate blind spots.
  • Minutes 23–40 · Tools & data classes: map 3 pilots max; forbid scope explosion; tie each to NZ privacy sensitivities (“customer PII?” “Māori data?”).
  • Minutes 41–52 · People plan: who must be persuaded internally; crib lines from Team worksheet.
  • Minutes 53–60 · Next step & Pilot: what they owe you for templates; clarify you don’t adjudicate subsidy—provide factual homework for Priority.

Within 48 hours ship: transcript link, concise bullet memo (tool stack reco), Pilot prep doc scaffold (bullet outline is fine)—then commence 30‑day asynchronous support with response-time norms you declare once.

Gold · suggested eight-week delivery scaffold
Adjust per client—but default rhythm prevents existential drift.
Week 0 · Kick-off
Stakeholder map, data inventories, appetite for Māori governance review.
Week 1–2 · Draft backbone
Adoption Plan outline circulated—exec summary first so leadership doesn’t drown.
Week 3–4 · Deep dives
Operationalise pilots referenced in worksheets; annotate risk mitigations.
Week 5 · Workshop rehearsal
Slides borrow from Sessions 3–4; tailor stories to attendee literacy.
Week 6 · Live workshop (~90 min)
Recording + attendee FAQ log becomes appendix.
Week 7–8 · Hardening
Final Adoption Plan pagination, appendix with Te Tiriti + copyright statements, sign-off rituals.

Fortnightly 30‑min check-ins should each end with captured decisions—“We committed to ___ by ___”—so Adoption Plan versioning isn’t ethereal.

Competitive framing (mental models—not trash talk)
Buyers silently compare you to substitutes; name them fairly.
  • DIY YouTube binge: cheap, zero accountability, no NZ law stitching—stress ongoing Q&A boundaries you enforce.
  • Corporate LLM licences sold by AU/NZ VARs: strong on SSO, weaker on Māori workforce nuance—you own cultural fluency plus worksheet choreography.
  • MBA-style strategy houses: expensive, slower; you occupy velocity + Pilot alignment niche if you defend documentation quality.
  • In-house IT: sometimes allies—position yourself as accelerating their backlog, not replacing headcount narratives.

Chapter 22
Troubleshooting · payments & transactional email
When adrenaline spikes, walk this list top-to-bottom.
  • Checkout returns 503: Stripe keys or Price IDs missing—server logs cite which tier lacked STRIPE_PRICE_TAURANGA_*.
  • Paid but no email: webhook not firing (endpoint URL, signing secret mismatch) OR Resend outage—Stripe dashboard event replay after fixing.
  • 404 on gated templates: random slug folders not deployed—see assets/README.md vs buildAccessLinks() actual URL shape.
  • Buyer says link expired: It didn’t—they lost email; validate identity via Stripe customer email before re-sending artefacts manually.
