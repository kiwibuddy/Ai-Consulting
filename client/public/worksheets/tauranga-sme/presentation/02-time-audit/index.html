The taxonomy

Every owner-operator hour falls into one of four buckets.

Sort your week into these four. The ratio is your business model. The compression target lives almost entirely in the first two.

1
Repeatable admin
Quoting, invoicing, scheduling, reporting. AI's strongest current ground. 60–80% achievable compression.
2
First-draft creative / writing
Proposals, marketing copy, social posts. Big speed-up — human review still required.
3
Decisions / judgement
Pricing, hiring, performance, customer escalations. AI assists, never decides.
4
Relationship / brand
In-person sales, partner relationships, on-site presence. Stays human.

The audit, walked

30 minutes. Three columns.
Three pilots.

Open the Time Audit worksheet (in your pack). Set a 30-minute timer. Don't optimise — answer the questions and move on. The compression goes on the second pass.

A
List the top 10 tasks you did this week
Owner only. Not the team. Use last Friday's calendar as a memory aid.
B
Tag each: Admin / Creative / Decision / Relationship
If you can't decide, default to Admin. The honest disagreement is the audit working.
C
Rank the Admin and Creative items by hours
Top three are your pilots. Pick a tool for each and book a 30-day review on the calendar before you close the worksheet.

A worked example

A real BoP owner, three pilots, 12 hours back.

Anonymised but representative. Trades business, 6 staff, owner-operator, December 2025 audit.

Before — owner's typical week

  • 11 hrs quoting, mostly evenings
  • 4 hrs invoicing / reconciling
  • 3 hrs subbie email triage
  • 2 hrs writing job-completion reports

After — three pilots, 30 days in

  • Quotes drafted from photos + voice notes (save 6 hrs)
  • Bank reconciliation auto-categorised (save 2 hrs)
  • Email triage rules + drafted replies (save 4 hrs)
  • Reports left as-is — already efficient
Net: 12 hrs/week recovered. Cost of tools ~$140 NZD/month. Owner now finishes evenings at home, not at the desk.
