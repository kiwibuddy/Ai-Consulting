Privacy Act 2020 · OPC guidance

If it processes personal info,
run a PIA first.

The Office of the Privacy Commissioner is unambiguous: every IPP applies to AI use. Senior leadership approval is expected. Human review is required before acting on AI outputs that affect a person.

PIA — what it covers

  • What personal info goes in (and where it ends up)
  • Whether the supplier trains on your data
  • Cross-border data flow (most AI tools are offshore)
  • How long data is retained
  • Who, internally, signs off on the deployment

Common SME failures

  • Pasting customer details into a public ChatGPT window
  • Using AI tools without a privacy review
  • No human-in-the-loop on AI-derived decisions
  • Customer-facing AI without disclosure
  • Training data including ex-employees' personal info

Copyright Act 1994 · NZ's anomaly

Yes, you (probably) own your AI-generated content.

NZ is internationally distinctive: the Copyright Act explicitly protects 'computer-generated works' that meet an originality test, even without a human author. But supplier contracts must reflect this — most haven't been updated.

What you should do

  • Add a 'works created using AI' clause to supplier contracts
  • Keep prompts and inputs as evidence of human originality
  • Avoid AI tools whose ToS claim ownership of your outputs
  • Disclose AI involvement to customers where ethically required

AJ Park's practical gaps

  • Training-data licensing in your supply chain
  • Output ownership in commercial contracts
  • Indemnity from AI vendors (often missing)
  • Trademark / logo creation needs separate review

Māori Data Sovereignty

Data is taonga.
The principles are clear.

For BoP businesses with iwi partners, customers in te ao Māori, or hiring from Toi Kai Rawa's pipeline, Māori Data Sovereignty isn't a nice-to-have. It's the same kind of obligation as the Privacy Act, with sharper expectations.

1
Identify Māori data in your business
Customer lists, employee data, supplier contacts, photos, narratives — Māori data is broader than 'iwi info'.
2
Apply MDS principles to AI tool selection
Where is the data stored? Who governs it? Does the tool train on it? Cross-border issues are heightened for Māori data.
3
Document the conversation
Even a single-page checklist showing you considered MDS in your tool selection is a meaningful step. Gold tier includes the template.
