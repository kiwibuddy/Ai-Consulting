The Analog Asset Log

A written inventory of what works in your house when networks, smart-home apps, and AI assistants don't.

Print at 100% · A4
Nathaniel Baldock · 2026

A surprising amount of a modern household runs through software now: locks, garage doors, heating, lighting, payments, identity, even key documents. This worksheet is the audit document for what still works when the software does not. Three sections: physical overrides for digital systems, offline copies of essential records, and a small reference library worth keeping in print. Fill it in once, review annually, store one copy at home and one with a trusted family member at distance.

If a household AI assistant or agent goes wrong

If a smart-home AI assistant begins issuing instructions that contradict your judgement, do three things in order: (1) clear its memory and conversation history, (2) disconnect it from networked controls (toggle Wi-Fi off if needed), (3) operate critical systems manually using the table above until you can verify the AI is back to safe behaviour. This is not paranoia — the Cloud Security Alliance's NIST AI Agentic Profile (2026) explicitly lists behavioural drift across delegation chains as a known agentic-AI risk.

ii.

Offline copies of essential records

In waterproof storage
Updated annually

Copies of essential records, in physical form, in a waterproof container. Not originals — copies. Useful at every level of disruption from internet outage to extended displacement. The Federal Reserve has called this an Emergency Financial First-Aid Kit (EFFAK) for over a decade; the framing is universal.

Useful even in normal life

Books worth having in print. Not survivalist doctrine — useful at every level from "internet outage" to "extended displacement," and useful even when none of those occur. Keep what you would actually open. Borrow from your library to test before buying.

What this is not

This worksheet is not a survivalist inventory. It does not include firearms, large fuel reserves, off-grid communications gear, or any of the bunker apparatus the framework explicitly warns against. The hard cases this aids are not extinction scenarios — they are ordinary infrastructure outages, displaced-by-disaster scenarios, and the slow-motion stress of a degraded information environment. Every item here aids you at every level.
