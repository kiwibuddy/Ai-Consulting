The 30-Day Onboarding Plan

A guided four-week sequence for households starting from zero.

Worksheet 01 / 04
Print at 100% · A4
Nathaniel Baldock · 2026

Most households who agree the AI risk picture deserves attention have the same problem: they don't know where to start. This worksheet sequences the most consequential Level 1 and Level 2 actions across four weeks. Nothing here is theatrical. Each day's task takes 15 minutes to two hours. By day 30 you will have done the substantive 80% of household-level resilience work, and you will know which longer-cycle items remain.

01
Week One Identity & the family
  • DAY 01 Buy two hardware security keys. YubiKey 5C NFC or Google Titan. ~NZ$80 each. The two-key rule: one primary, one backup, stored apart.
  • DAY 02 Enrol the keys on your primary email account. Email is the recovery channel for everything else. Do this account first.
  • DAY 03 Enrol the keys on your primary bank and government-identity portal. RealMe in NZ. Most major NZ banks now support hardware keys.
  • DAY 04 Choose a family safe phrase. 4–7 unrelated words. See the Safe Phrase Guide for selection rules.
  • DAY 05 Tell every family member over 10, in person. Never write it down. Practice once.
  • DAYS 06–07 Install Signal on every household device. Migrate the family group chat. End-to-end encrypted by default. Free.
02
Week Two Devices & financial first-aid
  • DAY 08 Encrypt every laptop hard drive. BitLocker on Windows, FileVault on Mac. Both built in. Free. ~30 minutes once initiated.
  • DAY 09 On phones: turn off location services for any app that doesn't need it. Disable advertising IDs. Settings → Privacy on iOS; Settings → Privacy → Ads on Android.
  • DAY 10 Open every AI assistant in the household. Clear conversation logs and saved context. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Siri, Alexa.
  • DAYS 11–12 Assemble the EFFAK kit. Photo IDs, birth certificates, insurance, prescription lists in waterproof storage, plus low-denomination cash. See the Analog Asset Log.
  • DAY 13 Confirm 3–6 months emergency reserves. If short, set a savings rate to close the gap.
  • DAY 14 Review insurance. Add identity-theft and cyber-fraud rider where available.
03
Week Three Information & community
  • DAY 15 Build a written list of 20 trusted human sources. Named journalists, researchers, government officials. Update quarterly. Replace social-feed scrolling.
  • DAY 16 Reduce passive social-media intake. Set time limits or remove apps from your phone home screen.
  • DAY 17 Audit your own voice and video footprint online. 3 seconds of audio is enough for voice cloning.
  • DAYS 18–19 Map your local network. Name 5 people in walking distance, 10 in your neighbourhood. Note their skills: medical, mechanical, food production, vehicles.
  • DAY 20 Make actual contact with one of them this week. A coffee, a text, a knock on the door.
  • DAY 21 Find your local emergency-management group online. NZ: civildefence.govt.nz to find your CDEM Group. Bookmark the page.
04
Week Four Practice & the longer work
  • DAY 22 Have the elderly-parents conversation. Bring the Safe Phrase Guide. Practice the protocol with them in person. Highest-leverage 30 minutes in this whole plan.
  • DAY 23 Choose one analog practice to do weekly. Sustained reading, longhand journaling, walking conversation, prayer.
  • DAY 24 Establish one screen-free time block per day. Family meal, evening hour, morning routine. Hold it.
  • DAYS 25–26 Standard household emergency supplies. Water, food, medications, torch, battery radio. 7-day baseline. Build to 14 over time. NZ: getready.govt.nz.
  • DAY 27 Identify the AI policy venue you can engage. School board, denomination, professional body, council submission. Pick one.
  • DAY 28 Write the submission, letter, or email. Send it.
  • DAYS 29–30 Review. What's done, what's outstanding. Schedule the longer items into your calendar. Set the quarterly audit reminder.
After day 30, what becomes ongoing

The 30-day plan covers the substantive 80%. The remaining 20% is a handful of longer-cycle items: hybrid-skill career planning (12–18 months), paying down shelter where possible, deepening the local network, sustained civic engagement, weekly contemplative practice. The Household Readiness Checklist (Worksheet 02) lays out that ongoing rhythm.

Six principles to keep in mind through the month

i.

This is no-regret work. Every action on this list aids you regardless of how AI risk plays out. None depends on a particular forecast being right.

ii.

Done beats elegant. A clumsy completed safe-phrase protocol protects your family. A perfect un-built one does not.

iii.

Do days in order; weeks can flex. The week-one identity work is foundation. Weeks 2–4 can swap by a day or two if life intrudes.

iv.

The hardest day is day 22. The elderly-parents conversation is awkward. Do it anyway. The asymmetric value is real: adults 60+ lost US$7.7B to fraud in 2025.

v.

Do not lecture. If a family member is reluctant, lead with the actions, not the framework. Most people resist the pitch but accept the practice.

vi.

Skipped days are normal. Aim for 25 of 30. Make a note where you skipped and circle back in week 5.

✕What this plan deliberately does not include

  • Liquidating retirement savings or investments on the basis of AI-doom forecasts. The empirical record on panic-selling during sustained stress is uniformly negative.
  • Relocating to off-grid or "bunker" arrangements that sever you from professional networks, congregational life, and community trust. Resilience research is unanimous: relational density predicts surviving disruption, not geographic isolation.
  • Stockpiling firearms, large fuel reserves, or survivalist apparatus. None of these address the actual Level 1–2 risks. All carry significant offsetting harms.
  • Lecturing friends and family about AI risk in ways that frame them as fools. The cost-benefit on relational capital is poor; the persuasion benefit is near zero.
  • Removing children entirely from AI tools. Skill in using AI well, including knowing when not to, is itself a moat. Supervised engagement beats blanket prohibition.
