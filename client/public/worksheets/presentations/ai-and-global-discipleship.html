What This Means for the Church

1
People arriving at church are pre-formed by Social Media and AI.
Formation and counter-formation are the starting point, not an add-on.
2
The relational model of church community is our greatest asset.
Every study shows the best AI outcomes in human-AI hybrid environments. That's what church community already is.
3
Relational skills are the most valuable skills.
Workers protected from displacement have tacit knowledge and relational capacity.
4
We need an AI framework, not just a policy.
Rooted in imago Dei, stewardship, and the Holy Spirit's work.
5
The 57/14 gap is our assignment.

Section 09 · For Every Believer

Equipping the Next Generation

1
Teach augmentation over automation
Stanford's key finding: the workers protected are those who use AI to amplify their skills, not replace them.
2
Name the AI companion issue directly
This is a pastoral care conversation, not just a lecture topic. Some people's closest relationships are with chatbots.
3
Strengthen relational skills as future-proof training
Tacit knowledge, emotional intelligence, conflict resolution. These are the skills AI can't replace.
4
Strengthen our emphasis on Hearing God's Voice
In all aspects of life, above AI. The Holy Spirit's voice remains the ultimate guide. We need to know the difference between algorithmic suggestion and divine leading.
