Why this works
Most family tech rules are handed down from above. They create compliance, not conviction. When your kids help write the agreement, they actually own it. And ownership changes everything.
What research shows
AI companions are now racing for your child's attachment and intimacy, not just their attention (Tristan Harris, 2025). This isn't a phone problem. It's a formation problem — and it requires a family response.

Step 01
Before you begin: set the table
This works best when it feels like a family conversation, not a meeting. Order pizza. Put phones in a pile in the middle. Tell your kids: "We're going to make some decisions together about how our family uses AI — and everyone gets a say." Then actually mean it.
1
Explain AI simply
Make sure everyone knows what AI actually is — Siri, ChatGPT, recommendation feeds. Five minutes is enough.
2
Ask, don't lecture
Start with curiosity. What do you use AI for? What do you like? What worries you? Let kids speak first.
3
Fill together
Go through each column as a family. Write what everyone agrees on. Disagreement is fine — talk it through.
4
Sign & post it
Everyone signs. Put it somewhere visible — the fridge, a noticeboard, wherever your family actually looks.

A word for parents
"The process of making this together is the point."
You don't need a perfect agreement. You need a real conversation. The fact that you sat down together, that your kids were heard, that they put their name on something — that is discipleship. It's Deuteronomy 6 lived out in an ordinary evening around the table.

AI will keep changing. Your agreement will need to change with it. But the habit of navigating hard things together as a family, with God in the room — that's what you're actually building here. And that lasts far longer than any technology will.
