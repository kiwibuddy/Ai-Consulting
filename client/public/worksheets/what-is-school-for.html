"What Is School
Actually For?"
A student reflection for the age of AI
If AI can already retrieve information, write essays, summarise textbooks, and generate code — the question isn't whether AI is useful. The question is: what does that leave for school to be? This worksheet asks you to think seriously about that — and write yourself a letter from the other side of the answer.

Part 1 — Start with the honest question
What is school for — if AI can do most of what school teaches?
"
"It's no longer about information. AI now gives you all the information you need. What we need to learn are people skills. I think education that teaches people skills is going to be some of the most important education in the next 15, 20, 30 years — because AI can't teach us relational skills."
Nathaniel Baldock — Spheres Teaching, University of the Nations

The word "education" comes from the Latin educere — which means "to draw out." Not "to put information in." To draw out what's already there. The idea is that every student has something inside them that school is supposed to surface and develop. That's a very different model from a data download — and it's the only model AI can't replicate.

Before you answer any of the questions below, sit with this for a moment: if AI had existed when your parents were at school, what would they have stopped learning? And what would they have missed by not having to struggle through it themselves?

A word to close
The goal of education — the real one, buried under the curriculum documents and assessment schedules — is to draw out what's already in you. Educere. To surface the person who is capable of building something, sustaining a friendship, telling the truth when it costs something, and showing up for people who need you.

AI will keep getting better at the information part. That's not going away. But it cannot want things for you. It cannot be shaped by failure the way you can. It cannot love the people in your life, and it cannot be loved back. Those are the irreducibly human things. School at its best has always been about making space for them to grow.

You get to decide what you do with the space.
