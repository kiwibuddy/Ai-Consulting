Part 1
The Influence Map
Write your child's name in the centre circle. Fill each ring with what is actually shaping them right now. Be specific — name the YouTubers, the games, the friends, the apps, the chatbots.
Map legend
Inner ring: Constant presence — people your child is with every single day
Middle ring: Regular contact — school, church, sport, weekly friends
Outer ring: Passive & algorithmic — apps, feeds, AI tools, media

Part 3
The Honest Questions
Don't rush through these. Give yourself real space to sit with each one.
1
When your child faces something hard — failure, loneliness, confusion, fear — what or who do they go to first?
2
What values is your child absorbing right now? Where are those values actually coming from?
3
Is there an influence on the map that surprised or unsettled you when you saw it written down? What is it, and why does it unsettle you?
4
When you imagined raising your child, what did you picture? Is this what you intended?

Conversation starters for your kids
Try one of these at dinner this week. No lecture. Just curiosity.
"Who do you think knows you best — us, your friends, or your phone?"
"When something goes wrong, what's the first thing you reach for?"
"If all the apps disappeared tomorrow, what would you miss most? Why?"
"What do you think those apps actually want from you?"
"Is there anything you'd rather talk to me about than Google it?"
A word of encouragement
Parenting has never been simple. And raising children in a world shaped by algorithms, AI companions, and always-on screens can feel like you're navigating territory no generation before you has faced. Some days that's genuinely overwhelming.

But here is what hasn't changed: God still speaks to parents. He still gives wisdom to those who ask. He still sees your children more clearly than any platform ever could. The fact that you're sitting with this worksheet is not a small thing. You are paying attention. You care. And that instinct to be intentional — to lean in rather than drift — is exactly what your kids need from you.

Not perfection. Not all the answers. Just a parent who is present, who keeps asking the hard questions, and who believes that God is still in the business of forming families that can be salt and light in whatever world their children grow up into.
