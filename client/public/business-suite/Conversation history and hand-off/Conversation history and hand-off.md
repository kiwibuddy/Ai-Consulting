Reading these together, five threads emerge that are bigger than any single article:  
**The hiring market has become machine-to-machine warfare, with humans squeezed out on both sides.** AI auto-apply tools flood the top of the funnel; AI screeners reject 75% of resumes before a human sees them; recruiters respond with more opaque filters. The result is a volume arms race optimizing for speed, not quality of hire — and 27 million capable people filtered out by proxy signals like employment gaps.  
**Trust has collapsed, so a verification economy is emerging.** Ghost jobs (1 in 3 listings), fabricated skills (67% of managers caught one), AI-polished resumes, and secret scoring have poisoned the well in every direction. The market response — verifiable credentials, evidence trails, integrity checks — is essentially the market rediscovering that *truthfulness is infrastructure*. When deception is normalized (40% of managers admit posting fake roles; 62% did it to make staff feel replaceable), honesty becomes a competitive advantage.  
**The capability-demand inversion is theologically loaded.** The SAFI data shows AI strongest at mathematics (73.2) and programming (71.8), weakest at active listening (42.2) and social perception. The skills machines can't replicate are precisely the *relational* ones — which lands exactly on the claim in your Economics corpus that relationship, not matter, is the foundational reality of the universe.  
**Augmentation, not replacement, is the dominant pattern — where humans stay in the loop.** 78.7% of workplace AI interactions are augmentation. HITL workflows improve quality 45–60%. The ICO's demand that human oversight be "active and genuine, not a rubber stamp" is a regulator accidentally articulating a theology of human agency.  
**Accountability is arriving through law, not ethics.** The Eightfold/Workday lawsuits attack the *process* of secret scoring — people judged by dossiers they can't see or dispute. Scripture's language for this is old: dishonest scales, judgment without a hearing, the worker denied wages. Regulation is catching up to what the Economics sphere purpose statement already says: gainful employment at a fair price, with integrity of the workforce.  
The human cost underneath all of it: futility. 9 hours wasted per ghost job, rejection in 0.3 seconds, "invisible forces" — a dignity crisis dressed up as an efficiency story.
