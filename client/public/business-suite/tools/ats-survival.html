What ATS systems actually do

Applicant tracking systems extract text from your file, map it to fields (job title, dates, employer), and match keywords. They are often "dumb filing cabinets" (Wonsulting, 2026) — design-heavy PDFs, two-column layouts, and headers in Word footers break parsing. Over 40% of résumés contain at least one formatting element that causes errors (JobWizard, 2026). You can be fully qualified and invisible.

Why .docx wins
PDFs often store text as graphic chunks. Word's linear XML stream preserves job titles and dates. Use PDF only when the employer portal explicitly requires it. Scale.jobs (2026)
Why columns break
Two-column layouts failed in 7 of 8 ATS tests — titles and dates jumble or merge into nonsense. QuickCV (2025)
Why date format matters
ResumeAdapter identifies standardised MM/YYYY as the single most critical parsing fix. Inconsistent formats cause blank employment history in recruiter view. ResumeAdapter (2026)
Safe header template
SUMMARY WORK EXPERIENCE EDUCATION SKILLS CERTIFICATIONS (optional)
Copy these exact headers into a plain Word document. No icons or text boxes.
Header/footer trap
Modern ATS parsers frequently ignore content placed in Word headers or footers — your phone number may never be extracted. JobWizard (2026)
What to remove
Skill meters, charts, logos, and multi-colour infographics scramble the text stream. Plain black text on white is the highest-parse format.
