The problem in plain language

ATS platforms are often "dumb digital filing cabinets" (Wonsulting, 2026) — they extract text, match keywords, and rank. Design-heavy PDFs, two-column layouts, and creative headers break parsing: over 40% of résumés contain at least one element that causes errors, sometimes leaving a blank profile in the recruiter's view (JobWizard, 2026). You can be fully qualified and invisible.

The 6-point survival checklist
1 · File format.docx default — Word outperformed PDF in 6 of 8 ATS tests (QuickCV, 2025). PDF only when explicitly requested.
2 · Single columnTwo-column layouts failed in 7 of 8 systems — titles and dates jumble together.
3 · MM/YYYY datesThe single most critical parsing fix (ResumeAdapter, 2026). Not "Jan 2020" or seasons.
4 · Standard headers"Work Experience", "Education", "Skills" — no icons, text boxes, or tables.
5 · No header/footer contentKey details in Word headers or footers are often ignored entirely.
6 · Safe fontsArial, Calibri, Georgia, Times New Roman. No skill meters or graphics.
Integrity rule (non-negotiable)
AI structures; you verify. 67% of hiring managers caught at least one fabricated skill on a résumé in the last twelve months (SkillUpArc, 2026). Never invent metrics — interview failure is worse than ATS silence.
