## What we believe about AI

These are not rules. They are convictions — things we hold to be true that shape
everything that follows.

**1. Human judgment is irreplaceable.** AI assists people; it does not replace human
judgment, relationships, or responsibility. The final decision, and the
accountability for it, stays with a person.

**2. People before efficiency.** We will not use AI in a way that degrades the
quality of human attention we owe the people we serve.

**3. Truth and verification.** We verify what AI produces before we rely on it, and
we never present AI-generated work as something it is not.

**4. Privacy and confidentiality.** Personal and confidential information about the
people we serve does not go into public AI tools — an ethical commitment and a
requirement of the NZ Privacy Act 2020.

**5. Shared accountability.** AI use is not a private matter each person navigates
alone. It is held together — set by leadership, owned by everyone.

## The policy

**Scope.** This policy applies to all paid staff, volunteers in significant roles,
and anyone acting on behalf of **[ORGANISATION]** in an official capacity. It covers
AI use in communications, administration, content creation, service delivery, and
decision support.

### What we welcome
- Drafting and researching communications, documents, and content — where a person
  reviews and approves the final output before it goes out in our name.
- Administrative tasks: summarising notes, organising information, drafting
  correspondence — where no confidential personal data is involved.
- Translation and language support.
- Learning, brainstorming, and exploring ideas.

### What we do not do
- Enter confidential or personal information about the people we serve into any
  public AI tool.
- Present AI-generated content as the original, unassisted work of a person.
- Use AI to replace the human care, presence, or judgment that is central to our work.
- Make significant decisions about people based solely on AI-generated analysis.
- Use AI tools that have not been approved for **[ORGANISATION]** use.

### What we are honest about
When AI meaningfully helped produce something, we say so. A brief acknowledgement is
enough. **The test:** if someone would feel misled to learn AI was used, we should
have been more transparent.

### Privacy and data
**[ORGANISATION]** holds personal information under the NZ Privacy Act 2020. Anything
typed into a public AI tool leaves our systems and enters the systems of large
technology companies. Personal details and confidential information must never be
processed this way. **When in doubt, leave it out.**

### Who is responsible
**[LEADERSHIP BODY]** holds collective responsibility for this policy. Every person
is personally responsible for their own AI use within it. If you are unsure whether a
use is appropriate, ask before you act.

## In practice: five questions

For day-to-day decisions, these are enough:

1. Does this involve personal or confidential information about anyone we serve? *If
   yes, do not use AI.*
2. Am I comfortable being transparent about AI's role here, if asked? *If not,
   rethink the use or disclose it clearly.*
3. Have I verified the content for accuracy and appropriateness? *If not, do that first.*
4. Is this replacing something that deserves human attention? *If yes, choose the
   person over the tool.*
5. Has this tool been approved for our use? *If not, check first.*
