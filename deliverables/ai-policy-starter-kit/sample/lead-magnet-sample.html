The one rule the whole kit hangs on: when in doubt, leave it out.

What we believe about AI — the five convictions

Every policy in the kit is built on five convictions. Here they are in full:

1. Human judgment is irreplaceable. AI assists people; it does not replace human judgment, relationships, or responsibility. The final decision — and the accountability for it — stays with a person.
2. People before efficiency. We will not use AI in a way that degrades the quality of human attention we owe the people we serve.
3. Truth and verification. We verify what AI produces before we rely on it, and never present AI-generated work as something it is not.
4. Privacy and confidentiality. Personal and confidential information about the people we serve does not go into public AI tools — an ethical commitment and a NZ Privacy Act 2020 requirement.
5. Shared accountability. AI use is not a private matter each person navigates alone. It is held together — set by leadership, owned by everyone.

The five questions to ask before you hit enter

  1. Personal or confidential info about anyone we serve? → If yes, stop.
  2. Comfortable being transparent about AI's role, if asked? → If not, rethink or disclose.
  3. Verified it for accuracy and appropriateness? → If not, do that first.
  4. Replacing human attention someone deserves? → If yes, choose the person.
  5. Approved tool? → If not, check first.

The AI Traffic Light — what data can go into AI?

LevelExamplesWhat to do
🟢 GREEN — safe General, non-personal, already-public information Go ahead. A person still reviews the output.
🟡 AMBER — caution Internal but not sensitive; de-identify first Strip names/identifying details before you paste.
🔴 RED — never Personal, confidential, or legally protected information Never enter into a public AI tool. Privacy Act 2020.
