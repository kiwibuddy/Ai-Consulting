# Cover letter template (consultant → client)

**Sales / fulfilment layer — NOT part of the $500 self-serve Basic kit.** Use this when
sending a drafted policy to a prospect or client as the covering note for a facilitated
or Ai Plus engagement. Everything in **[square brackets]** is a fill-in. Keep it warm and
short; the goal is a conversation, not a pitch.

---

[Date]

Dear **[Name(s)]**,

I've been putting something together for **[ORGANISATION]** that I think could be
genuinely useful for your team — and I wanted you to see it before anyone else does.

Your staff are almost certainly already using AI. Most **[sector, e.g. teams / church
teams / organisations]** are — for **[typical uses, e.g. drafting, communications,
research, admin]**. That's fine. It isn't a crisis to manage; it's a reality to get
ahead of. What tends to happen without a shared framework is that each person makes
their own call about what's appropriate, which creates real risk — usually invisibly,
until it doesn't.

So what I've put together is a draft **AI Policy for [ORGANISATION]**. Written in your
language and short enough that people will actually read it and remember it. It includes
a practical **Traffic Light System** that gives your team a simple decision tool for any
AI task — not just a list of rules that lives in a folder.

**One thing worth flagging.** AI meeting assistants like Otter.ai and Fireflies can join
video calls automatically, sometimes without anyone quite realising. If one ends up in a
**[confidential — e.g. pastoral, HR, safeguarding, client]** conversation, that's a real
problem. The policy addresses it directly, but it's worth your team knowing regardless of
what you do with the document.

**A legal update worth knowing about.** A new Information Privacy Principle — IPP 3A —
came into force on 1 May 2026. Where personal information is collected indirectly (for
example when **[someone registers another person / a third party provides details]**),
there is now generally an obligation to take reasonable steps to make sure that person is
notified. It's worth a brief look at whether your privacy documentation needs an update,
and a conversation with a legal adviser about exactly what applies to your intake flows.
I've flagged it in the policy draft.

**[Optional "first in your network" hook:]** As far as I can tell, there's no AI
governance document anywhere in **[the client's network / sector locally]** right now. If
**[ORGANISATION]** adopts something like this, you'd be among the first — and I think that
matters. The people you serve trust you with some of the most sensitive information in
their lives. That trust deserves a framework.

I've enclosed **[N]** things with this letter:

- **The AI Policy draft** — written for **[ORGANISATION]**, ready for your team to review,
  adapt, and adopt.
- **Website AI statements (four options)** — if you want to be transparent publicly about
  how you use AI, with honest notes on the risks and benefits of publishing.
- **A short proposal for follow-up work** — if your team would benefit from help
  implementing this. The staff training session in particular tends to be where things
  actually change.

No pressure on any of it. The policy draft is yours to do whatever you want with. But I do
think getting your team around a table for even a half-day would make a real difference —
people walk away knowing what to do and what not to do. That's worth a lot.

I'm based in **[location]**, so I'm easy to connect with if you'd like to talk any of it
through. My details are below.

Warmly,

**Nathaniel Baldock**

nathanielbaldock.com · **[email]** · **[location]**
