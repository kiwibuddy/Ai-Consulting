## Why this policy exists (reference draft)
Our calling has not changed: to love and serve the people God brings to us. AI is a
tool that can help us with sermon prep, communications, and admin — but it cannot
pray, intercede, listen for God's voice, or bear witness to Christ. This document
makes our approach to AI visible, accountable, and grounded in who we are.

## Public vs Secure (Enterprise) tools
Churches often have both a free consumer account and a managed one. Make the
distinction explicit so Amber-category work has a safe home:
- **Public AI tools** — free consumer ChatGPT / Claude etc. that may train on what you
  type. Assume anything typed here could become public. **Green-category tasks only.**
- **Secure (enterprise) sandbox** — a church-managed account such as Google Workspace /
  **Gemini for Nonprofits**, with zero-retention or private-data terms. Your work stays
  in-house and is not used to train the model. **Approved for Amber-category tasks.**

## Māori data sovereignty (add to Privacy)
Recognise that for Māori whānau, personal information is taonga — its use subject to
tino rangatiratanga and kaitiakitanga (aligned with Te Mana Raraunga principles).
