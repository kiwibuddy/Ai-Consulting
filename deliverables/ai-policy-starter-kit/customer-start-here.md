# AI Policy Starter Kit — Start Here

Thank you for your purchase. This kit gives you everything you need to adopt a clear,
plain-language AI policy your whole team will actually read.

## What's in your download

| File | What it is |
|------|-----------|
| `01-ai-policy-template.docx` | Your editable AI policy — open in Word or Google Docs and fill in the bracketed fields |
| `02-traffic-light-data-guide.pdf` | A one-page Green / Amber / Red guide to pin up or add to your staff handbook |
| `03-staff-acknowledgement-form.docx` | A short form for each staff member and key volunteer to sign |
| `05-website-ai-statements.docx` | Four ready-to-adapt public statements about how you use AI — from a one-line footer note to a full page — plus an honest brief on whether to publish |
| `sectors/…` | Four tailored intros — pick the one that fits you (business, church, not-for-profit, or school) |

## Five steps to adopt it

1. Open the **sector intro** that fits you and read the framing and examples.
2. Open `01-ai-policy-template.docx`. Work through it with your leadership team.
   Anything in **[square brackets]** is a prompt for you to write in your own voice.
3. Fill in your **Approved AI Tools** table and pin up the **Traffic Light** guide.
4. Have every staff member and key volunteer sign the **Acknowledgement Form**.
5. Adopt it, date it, and review it once a year.
6. *(Optional)* If you want to be transparent publicly, adapt one of the four
   **Website AI Statements** — the brief inside helps you decide whether to publish.

## The one rule to remember

When in doubt, leave it out. Never put personal or confidential information about the
people you serve into a public AI tool.
